# Social Pressures

Use complications sparingly, when the scene needs a push.

- The hearing chair may rush the vote if the room feels unstable.
- The guild patron may offer a private favor or apply quiet pressure.
- The worker delegate may lose patience if the workers are managed instead of heard.
- The junior clerk may reveal something indirectly before he risks saying it aloud.
- The temple archivist may confirm that the record deserves scrutiny if there is a procedural
  reason to ask her.
- Rumors can move faster than facts.

Consequences should be social first: attitude shifts, reputation changes,
favors, stress, delays, public commitments, or closed doors.
