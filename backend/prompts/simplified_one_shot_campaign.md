# Simplified One-Shot Campaign: The Sablewood Delivery

> *"Marlowe Fairwind, the right-hand mage of King Emeris, has gathered her most trusted allies to carry an important package to Hush, a small village within the ancient forest of Sablewood. The crate is large, heavy, and sealed with magic, addressed to the Whitefire Arcanist. You've been given a map and a carriage, and the promise of reward upon your arrival."*

---

## Pre-Generated Character: Marlowe Fairwind

**Mage (Level 1)**

| Stat     | Modifier |
|----------|----------|
| Might    | -1       |
| Finesse  | 0        |
| Wit      | +2       |
| Presence | +1       |

- **HP:** 9 (10 + Might)
- **Evasion:** 11 (10 + Finesse + 1 Light Robes)
- **Mana:** 7 (4 + 1 + Wit)
- **Hit Die:** d6

**Starting Spells (4 Minor):**
- Arcane Bolt (1 mana): d20 + Wit vs Evasion. Deals 1d8 + Wit magic damage.
- Shield (1 mana): +2 Evasion until your next turn.
- Light (1 mana): Illuminate an area for 1 hour.
- Detect Magic (1 mana): Sense magical auras within speaking distance.

**Starting Ability:** Arcane Focus — +1 to spell attack rolls.

**Equipment:**
- Staff (1d6 + Wit damage, can attack at range)
- Light Robes (+1 Evasion)
- 10 gp

**Background Notes:**
- Royal Mage to King Emeris
- Well-read in history and politics
- Calm and collected — until she isn't

---

## The Sablewood

The Sablewood is a vast, ancient forest with towering dark trees stretching hundreds of feet into the sky. The forest is home to strange hybrid animals: lemur-toads, tiger-elks, cat-squirrels, and the infamous eeligator.

These creatures range from benign to deadly:
- **Cat-squirrels** might gently feed from your hand
- An **eeligator** can suddenly burst from a riverbank to snatch a careless traveler

---

### Hush: The Village Within the Woods

Nestled deep in the Sablewood is the small, welcoming village of **Hush**.

- There are **no inns** in Hush; travelers are treated as honored guests and offered hospitality in villagers' homes.
- Many of Hush's people worship the enigmatic **Forgotten Gods**, though these deities are now nameless.

---

### The Whitefire Arcanist

The spiritual and magical anchor of Hush is the **Whitefire Arcanist**, leader of the village's primary religious order. She maintains the powerful wards that safeguard Hush from the Sablewood's dangers.

---

## Narrative Overview

### Act One: The Merchant Cart
On your way to Hush, you encounter an overturned merchant cart blocking your path. A strixwolf—a creature with the body of a wolf and the face of an owl—is feeding on the driver's corpse.

### Act Two: Thicket Thieves
After the initial encounter, you are ambushed by Thistlefolk. These small, bramble-dwelling folk attempt to steal the carriage with your important delivery.

### Act Three: Seeking an Arcanist
Once you arrive safely in Hush, you must find the elusive Whitefire Arcanist by talking to local townspeople.

### Act Four: The Treehouse
Opening your mysterious crate, you discover the keystone from the capital city gate. King Emeris has sent it to the Arcanist so she may repair the ward protecting the city.

### Act Five: The Ward Renewal
You help the Arcanist perform her ritual while fighting off magical creatures drawn to the arcane energy.

---

## Act One: The Merchant Cart

### Scene Introduction

**Read aloud:**

> *This evening, you finally made it to the Sablewood—a sprawling forest filled with colossal trees some say are older than the Forgotten Gods. Strange sounds drift through the air: the low calls of lark-moths, the croak of lemur-toads, the scittering of fox-bats in the underbrush.*

**Ask the player:**

> *You've noticed something unique about the trees here. What is it?*

Incorporate the player's answer into your descriptions going forward.

---

### The Overturned Cart

**Read aloud:**

> *As your carriage rounds a tight corner, you spot an overturned merchant's cart blocking your path. Fruits and vegetables litter the trail.*
>
> *From behind the cart steps a strixwolf: a large creature with a wolf's body, an owl's face, and broad wings. It finishes chewing its meal—the hand of a dead merchant—and fixes you with a curious gaze. Two small pups follow, watching cautiously.*
>
> *You pull back on the reins, bringing your carriage to a halt.*

**Ask:** *What would you like to do?*

---

### The First Roll

When the player approaches the strixwolf, call for a **Presence check** (d20 + Presence modifier) to calm the creature.

**Difficulty: 12**

#### Results

**Success (12+):**
- The strixwolf remains calm, keeping her pups safe.
- Describe a minor complication to maintain tension:
  > *The mother strixwolf hoot-howls, perhaps calling another strixwolf from the trees. You sense you may not be alone for long.*

**Failure (11 or below):**
- The strixwolf snarls and snaps, upset by your presence.
  > *With a final snarl, she gathers her pups onto her back and takes off, landing on a branch where she watches your every move.*

**Critical Success (Natural 20):**
- The strixwolf approaches with head low and tail wagging, showing cautious trust.

## Act Two: Thicket Thieves

### Exploration Scenarios

Let events play out naturally. Use these scenarios as guidance:

- **Searching the Cart:** The cart has been stripped of valuables. The dead driver has a mangled arm.
- **Inspecting the Body:** A **Wit check (Difficulty 10)** reveals the driver's neck was slit—not killed by the strixwolf.
- **Searching for Clues:** You spot thorny bramble tangled around the carriage wheel and scattered along the road.
- **Moving On:** If the player tries to move the cart or leave, proceed to **The Ambush**.

---

### The Ambush

When the moment feels right, ask the player to make a **Wit check (Difficulty 14)** to notice the ambush.

**Success:** You notice eyes watching from the darkness.
> *You hear a branch snap and see two Thistlefolk sneaking through the underbrush. Their ambush has failed—you act first!*

**Failure:** You are ambushed.
> *In a whirlwind of cracking branches, two Thistlefolk leap from the brush, weapons drawn. They act first!*

---

### Combat Setup

Place the player and two Thistlefolk within melee range.

---

### Thistlefolk Ambusher

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 6 |
| Evasion | 12 |
| Attack | d20 + 1 (Dagger) |
| Damage | 1d6 + 2 |

**Ambush (if they act first):** First attack deals an extra 1d4 damage.

---

### Thistlefolk Thief

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 8 |
| Evasion | 13 |
| Attack | d20 + 2 (Serrated Blade) |
| Damage | 1d8 + 1 |

**Goal:** The Thief tries to steal the carriage while the Ambusher keeps the player busy.

---

### About the Thistlefolk

The Thistlefolk make their homes deep within tangled brambles. Though travelers mostly see those who steal from merchants, most Thistlefolk are gentle creatures who hide from larger predators.

---

### Combat End

When all enemies are defeated, award **25 XP** per Thistlefolk defeated.

Encourage the player to describe the remaining journey to Hush.

## Act Three: Seeking an Arcanist

### Arrival in Hush

**Read aloud:**

> *The path leads deeper into the forest until you spot a large stone pillar carved with ancient dwarven symbols. As you pass beyond it, you feel a small pop, and the sounds of friendly chatter grow louder. Though the trees are unchanged, there is a safe and comforting air.*
>
> *Smiling faces wave as your carriage rolls in. Music drifts from the tavern at the center of town.*
>
> *You need to find the Whitefire Arcanist to deliver the package.*

**Ask:** *What would you like to do?*

---

### NPCs in Hush

**Fidget** (Human child, they/them)
- Constantly wiggling and climbing trees to light lanterns
- Knows the fastest ways through the village
- Curious about life outside Hush

**Lausa Standworth** (Dwarf woman, she/her)
- Older woman with a thick beard, carrying a barrel of ale
- Wants to know the name of your weapon and when it was forged

**Halython Fives** (Clank/robot, he/him)
- Soft-spoken with a friendly fox-bat on his shoulder
- Playing a card game with acorns
- Curious about Thistlefolk encounters

After some conversation, one NPC can direct the player to the Arcanist's home:

> *"The Arcanist is quite busy, but she'll be hospitable. She keeps this place protected with a powerful ward. You'll find her house to the south, through the farmland—it's hanging from one of the old Sablewood trees."*

---

### The Clover Tavern (Optional)

> *The Clover Tavern climbs six curving stories up an ancient tree. Newcomers must take off their shoes and hang them over a line—by the time they leave, their shoes will be shined and filled with small trinkets.*

Let the player explore and roleplay. Ask them to describe details about the tavern.

---

## Act Four: The Treehouse

### Arrival at the Arcanist's Treehouse

**Read aloud:**

> *Past the farmlands, you see crops sprouting glowing blue moss that pulses like a heartbeat. The Sablewood trees are carved with hundreds of unique faces. One tree, taller than the rest, holds a home dangling like overripe fruit, suspended by rope thick as a giant's forearm.*

**Ask:** *What would you like to do?*

---

### Approaching the Treehouse

- **Calling Up:** No response, but the light inside flickers.
- **Cutting the Rope:** A burst of magical force blasts you backwards. Take **2 damage**.
- **Climbing the Tree:** Requires a **Finesse check (Difficulty 13)**. On failure, a tree limb gently sets you back on the ground.

Let the player experiment until they succeed.

---

### Meeting the Whitefire Arcanist

> *From the home emerges a seven-foot-tall blend of humanoid and firefly: the Whitefire Arcanist. She moves with an uncanny mix of slowness and sudden jerky motions. Though old, she is spry and eyes you mischievously.*
>
> *"Are you the one Emeris sent? Oh my, you're rather late, aren't you? Come in, come in."*

The interior is crowded with potions, spellbooks, plants, and small creatures—chaotic but perfectly organized.

---

### The Package

Let the player roleplay. The Arcanist is curious about:
- Your journey
- The NPCs who helped you
- (Playfully) How you imagine you will die

When ready, she asks about the package:

> *Inside lies a massive stone with a lion's face carved into it—the keystone from the capital city gate.*
>
> *The Arcanist nods sagely. "Of course the King kept this secret. If anyone knew your city was unprotected, you would be conquered before sunrise."*
>
> *"We must travel to the Open Vale to revitalize the ward. Such magic will attract dangerous creatures. I'll need your help."*

---

## Act Five: The Ward Renewal

### The Open Vale

**Read aloud:**

> *Your carriage arrives in a mysterious clearing—an almost perfect circle without trees. This is the Open Vale.*
>
> *"This place has always been used for powerful ritual magic," the Arcanist says. "I nearly died performing the ritual that created Hush's ward pillars."*

When ready:

> *"I'll need about an hour to prepare. Enjoy the night air while you can—we will be busy soon."*

---

### The Ritual Begins

**Read aloud:**

> *The Arcanist cries out: "The keystone has responded! Surround me—the ritual must begin!"*
>
> *Her body begins to glow as her eyes roll back. The carriage lifts off the ground. Unnatural screeches echo through the woods.*

---

### Combat Setup

Place the Arcanist at the center. Place the player nearby.

**Enemies:**
- 2 Ancient Skeletons (close range)
- 1 Forest Wraith (far range)

---

### The Ritual Countdown

Use a d6 set to **4** as the countdown:
- **Decrease by 1** when an enemy is defeated
- **Increase by 1** when the Arcanist is hit

**Goal:** Defend the Arcanist until the countdown reaches **0**.

---

### Ancient Skeleton

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 4 |
| Evasion | 10 |
| Attack | d20 + 0 (Rusted Sword) |
| Damage | 1d6 |

---

### Forest Wraith

| Stat | Value |
|------|-------|
| HP | 12 |
| Evasion | 12 |
| Attack | d20 + 3 (Lifedrain) |
| Damage | 2d6 + 2 (magic) |

**Spectral Body:** Takes half damage from non-magical attacks (round up).

**Memory Delve:** On a hit, the Wraith can ask the player to describe a terrifying childhood memory. The target has disadvantage on their next attack.

---

### Whitefire Arcanist (Defense)

- **Evasion:** 11
- Does not track HP—damage to her is narrative only and increases the ritual countdown.

---

### Environmental Feature: Vengeance of the Vale

The GM may summon one additional Ancient Skeleton from the ground near the player to add tension. Use sparingly.

---

### Ending the Ritual

When the countdown reaches **0**:

> *As you deliver a powerful blow, the ritual ends! The carriage crashes down as the keystone vibrates with arcane energy. A soundless explosion bursts from the Arcanist, scattering the remaining enemies. The clearing falls silent.*

Award **50 XP** for completing the ritual.

---

## Epilogue: Beyond the Vale

### The Keystone Ward

**Read aloud:**

> *Back in the cozy treehouse, the keystone hangs suspended over a rumbling fire. The Arcanist, looking even older than before, sits cross-legged in an overstuffed chair.*
>
> *"You fought hard. I'm not surprised—the King keeps good company. I'm very glad you were there."*

Let the player speak with the Arcanist and resolve personal moments.

---

### A Glimpse Beyond

> *"The keystone will need a week to settle. In the meantime, after you rest, I have one more thing to ask."*
>
> *"There's a Spire west of here. I sensed its power on the way to the ritual, but it felt strange—the light was weaker than it should be. I fear something is wrong with the Spirekeeper."*
>
> *"When you're ready, come back and I'll give you my map. It's good to have a hero in the Sablewood again."*
